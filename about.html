The History of Colegio Sto. Domingo

1981

Mrs. Fe De Guzman, a noted educator from Jose Abad Santos Memorial School (JASMS), established the Fe De Guzman Tutorial Center (FGTC).

1984

Founded on the need for more individualized instruction in the pre-school level, FGTC became Fe De Guzman Learning Center (FGLC) which was spurred on by then Ministry of Education, Culture and Sports (now DepEd).

1996

After 12 years, FGLC introduced its Grade School Program. In hardly a year of its operation, FGLC Grade School was the first in Rizal to introduce Information Technology as part of its curriculum. The school experienced massive growth and as a result classes were transferred to a spacious building on Bedford Street.

2000

FGLC changed its name to Colegio Sto. Domingo. In the same year, the High School Department was introduced and the first batch of High School students were welcomed.

2002

Thanks to Sir Rolly De Guzman, CSD attains the four story building it currently is located at.

2003

The secondary department evolved into CSD Business High School. Marketing, Advertising, Accounting and Business Management electives strengthened the school’s vision and mission. The High School students apply all their learnings from their business electives at the annual Teenpreneur program where they are tasked to conceptualize or innovate, produce or improve, market and sell their products.

2009

The CSD Business High school department is renamed to Business and Science High school.

2016

To keep up with global standards, CSD, together with Eduspec. introduces Robotics as a club. Starting school year 2017-2018.

2020

To ensure accessibility to education, even in the midst of the pandemic, Colegio Sto. Domingo introduces Connections Rewired for Accessible Distance Learning Experiences (CRADLE).

2021

Colegio Sto. Domingo BSHS gets recertified by PEAC, Displaying CSD’s capability in keeping up with DepEd’s standards.

2024

In this year, CSD holds its 40th anniversary with CSD’s Got Talent, where students and staff show off their talents and skills. CSD goes through PEAC recertification once more, and maintains its status as a PEAc certified school. This will last until 2028, wherein the school will once again have to go through another recertification. With renewed vigor in its perspectives, a more powerful thrust in academic excellence, backed up by dedicated and competent faculty members, Colegio Sto. Domingo is now doing more than to answer a need in the community, it is now leading the way in answering the questions of the future.

Mission & Vision

Mission

Promote students' moral, social, spiritual, and personal development as inspired by the Divine love of God and the great integrity of St. Dominic. Uphold the importance of strong school-family partnership in molding its learners to reach their full potential. Provide distinctive quality education that equips learners with the skills necessary for a rapidly changing globalized world.

Vision

A well established student-centered institution in the country that provides exceptional education and develops happy, confident learners so they can be true nation-builders and agents of change for the community, the country, and the world

Incorruptible Character

Responsible Stewards

Dutiful Citizens
